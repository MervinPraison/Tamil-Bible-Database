Part 4 - Punctuation

This section contains the specification of punctuation standards and an easier method to input them

The importance of Punctuation warrants a separate section dedicated solely for it.

Types of punctuation to note:

The following are the types of punctuation to be corrected or checked when you are editing.

Quotation Marks

“ ” ‘ ’

Please note that the above listing does not include following ordinary quotes that you enter in your keyboard:

" " ' '

The position of double quotes (“ ”), play an important function in coloring Christ's words in red. Hence please be careful in editing chapters of the Gospel and Acts. Misplaced quotes would mess up the entire coloring scheme.

There is one exception to this rule, within Christ's words, when Christ is quoting another person (inside a parable for example,) irrespective of how they are printed, those quotes should be ordinary double quotes only.

As of now, Gospel and Acts have red lettering in them. I have completed red letter coloring for all of those verses that need them. I verified them three times. But I still could have missed something. So if you find any mistake in the red coloring here in this site please do let me know.

Red lettering cannot be previewed while coding in the web app.

Dashes

- – —

These three dashes are hyphen (-), en dash (–), em dash (—); I cannot find any consistency in usage of these dashes in the printed version of the bible. For time being we will use hyphen for nouns (names of places and people with two parts in them) and em dash only when the printed bible uses it. en dash is suppose to be used in cross reference but currently we are not following that standard.

Others

; : .

These are other ordinary punctuation symbols to be noted.
